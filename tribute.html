Dr. T. L. Osborn

The man who saved many souls.

Here's a time line of Dr. T.L. Osborn' life:


  • 1923 - Dr. Tommy Lee Osborn, Born in Grady County, Oklahoma

  • 1930 - When Osborn was six years old, His father who was a farmer moved to skedee,Oklahoma during the depression in search of a more prifitable farm. Osborn served his father deligently in their farm.

  • 1937 - Osborn had experienced a Christian conversion, at the age of 13, when his older brother took him to a Pentecostal church in Mannford. Gradually, each of his six brothers moved out of the family home until TL was the only boy still living with his parents and helping his 60-year-old father on the potato farm.

  • 1941 - Aged 15, Osborn was milking the cows when he began to cry. He fell on his knees, praying and asking God what was happening. "The Lord", he said, called him to be an evangelist, while he laughed and cried at the same time, overwhelmed by what was happening to him.

  • 1941 - Osborn was organizing evangelical meeting across Califonia when he met Daisy Washburn, in Los Banos, California in one of the evangelical meetings. It was 1941 and he was only 17 when he fell instantly in love.

  • 1942 - On April 5, 1942, Osborn married graduating high school student and farmer's girl, Daisy Washburn Osborn (born September 23, 1924 in Merced, California). He was 18, and she was only 17. In time, they carried the Gospel of Christ to tens of millions of people all over the world, declaring it with faith and confidence.

  • 1947 - In 1947, the Osborns had their only daughter, LaDonna Osborn.

  • 1948 - Osborn first gained public notice shortly after returning from India, as an evangelist on the Big Tent Revival circuit in the United States and Canada. Other young contemporary evangelists, including Oral Roberts, Billy Graham, Jack Coe, R.W. Schambach and A.A. Allen, were also on the circuit.

  • 1951 - By the early 1950s, their emphasis to shift more and more toward international missions. They held large crusades in Latin America, Asia, and Africa and crowds grew rapidly, at times exceeding 100,000.

  • 1957 - After Osborn's crusades in Thailand in 1956 and Uganda in 1957, Pastor Fred Wantaate of Makerere Full Gospel Church said that "after that crusade in Mombasa, the fountain of the river of Pentecostalism spread in the heart of East Africa".

  • 1958 - Over the course of the next five decades, Osborn and his team traveled to more than 70 countries healing and reached millions of people.

  • 1995 - Osborn's wife of 53 years, Daisy Osborn, died in Tulsa, Oklahoma, on May 27, 1995, at age 70. Thereafter, he continued to travel and conduct crusades around the world for another 15 years.

  • 2013 - Osborn died on February 14, 2013, at the age of 89. Osborn was survived by his daughter, three of four grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

"Dr. Tommy Lee Osborn was a great man of God who preached and practicalized the love of God to humanity with great passion and confidence. He emphasized the love and compassion of God, rather than the "fire and brimstone" theology style commonly used by evangelists of the era, and Dr. T.L. Osborn practiced supernatural healing in their meetings. The world missed him."

--Priced Evangelist Oyebola Daramola, Evangelist Nigeria
